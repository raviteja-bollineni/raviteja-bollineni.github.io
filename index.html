Project 2: Mechanical Lifting Assembly Design

A mechanical design task focused on developing a compact lifting assembly capable of raising a steel payload by 500 mm at a target speed of at least 100 mm/s. The design was created around a constrained base frame, where the assembly could only mount to the four M10 bosses provided on the top of the frame.

The final concept uses a motor-driven lead screw mechanism supported by dual guide rails to control vertical motion and reduce twisting during lifting. The design includes a bridge plate, linear guide supports, motor mounting, and a lifting carriage for the payload interface.

Tools: 3D CAD, STEP assembly design, mechanical calculations, lead screw selection, motor selection, lifting mechanism design

Key calculations: 31.6 kg payload, approximately 310 N lifting force, 1605 lead screw with 5 mm pitch, 1200 RPM motor speed, and 100 mm/s lift speed.

Project 3: BolliTherm BRT — Modular Thermal Battery

A self-initiated mechanical design concept for a modular thermal battery system intended to recover waste heat from exhaust streams and reuse it for steam generation or water heating. The concept explores how recovered thermal energy can reduce energy costs and support lower-carbon industrial operation.

The design includes a rectangular outer housing, internal thermal core arrangement, inlet and outlet connections, and controlled internal spacing to support heat transfer and modular assembly. The concept was developed to communicate an early-stage product idea focused on energy recovery, sustainability, and practical industrial integration.

Tools: 3D CAD, mechanical concept design, thermal system layout, modular product design, sustainability-focused engineering

Design focus: waste heat recovery, exhaust heat reuse, steam/water heating, modular thermal storage, energy cost reduction, and net-zero support.
